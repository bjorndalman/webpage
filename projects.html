High frequency filter construction

Saab AB, Microwave Engineering

The design project focused on creating an efficient down-converter to transform a 2 GHz signal to 200 MHz. It began with an input stage featuring a Low Noise Amplifier and included the design of two types of RF-filters: a side-coupled and a folded side-coupled filter. The mixer stage combined LO-signal and M-signal to produce an IF-signal, which was then filtered using a Chebyschev bandpass filter for minimal ripple. Finally, the processed signals were amplified and housed in shielded boxes, with the entire system built on a Duroid substrate and manufactured by Cogra Company.

Exhaust Gas Aftertreatment EU6

Volvo AB, Automotive Engineering

The main engineering goal is to enhance diagnostic capabilities for the complex Euro VI system, moving from basic sensor checks to predictive, real-time fault tracing. This involves integrating data from subsystems like EGR, DPF, and SCR to pinpoint issues rather than just symptoms. By developing On-Board Diagnostic (OBD) logic that uses pressure differential sensors, we create performance models that can predict problems before emissions limits are breached.
